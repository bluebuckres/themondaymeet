The Monday Meet

Sometimes, the most beautiful stories begin in the most ordinary places.

October 2025

Hi,

I'm writing this because in the rush and crowd of daily life, the right moments to just... talk... never seem to come. And I believe you deserve a better introduction than a clumsy "hello" shouted across a crowded bus.

Every Monday, I do the same old commute from Bagnan to my workplace. But a couple of months ago, that boring routine got... interesting. The day I first saw you, I didn't realize that a simple commute would start feeling… different.

I still remember that Monday — I boarded at the Bagnan bus stand, busy settling my bag, and didn't even realize you were there until I sat down. You were sitting on the left side, a few seats ahead. The bus was packed, people everywhere, and that day you looked like you were trying to stay lost in your sleepy dreams — or maybe just pretending to avoid the day. (I know how Mondays feel)

The whole ride, I was trying to figure out a way to say hi without sounding like a total weirdo. But... 👉👈

I don't know much about you — maybe you study BBA at JIS?
(If Feluda saw my detective skills, he'd probably shake his head and say, "Topse, tor dara kichu hobe na.")

Still, even without knowing the details, there was something about your presence — that calm, genuine, natural energy. It lingered with me for days. I don't even know why. For the first time in a long while, I thought, "Maybe she's someone I'd really like to know."

So, Who Am I?

Hey again — Supriyo here. From Bagnan too — guess that's something we already have in common.
I work in fintech now, but there's still a lot I'm figuring out.

You'd think one social media profile could handle introductions, but I'm more of a "talk in person" kind of guy.
No tea, no coffee — I know, I'm already breaking hearts. I just believe real connections don't need caffeine to spark.

Why This Website?

Okay, why a whole website? I know — it's a bit… extra.
But honestly, I couldn't think of any other way. Imagine trying to talk to someone on a packed bus — every uncle and auntie's eyes would be on us before I even finished my first sentence. Not ideal, right?

And what's the probability of getting a seat next to you?
I actually did the math — it's somewhere around 0.63%. (Yes, I'm that dedicated. "Desperate" just sounds bad. 😅)

So this felt like the only respectful way — a quiet corner of the internet where I can finally say what I've been wanting to, and you can read it whenever you like. No rush, no awkward glances — just a quiet page.

But What Am I Looking For?

Nothing much. I just want a chance for a real, simple conversation.
Maybe a walk somewhere in Kolkata — somewhere with a bit of green around us.
We could talk about serious world issues… like why Mondays exist, and how one person somehow made mine completely bearable.

You can tell me about your dreams, I'll bore you with stories from work, and we'll see where the talk takes us.

I just felt something genuine in those small moments, and I'd love to see if it's real. If it is — amazing. If not — at least we'll know we tried.

I just wonder sometimes — could the person who accidentally made my Monday better end up meaning a little more?

So... How Do We Beat the 0.63% Odds?

Easy — same bus, next Monday.
I'll be in my usual spot; you bring your lucky timing. Maybe we'll finally push those odds into triple digits.

Wait — are you actually blushing? That's a win for me already.
(Though I might have to ask for that smile back in person.)

Who knows — maybe one day I'll owe you that seat beside me… for a lifetime.

What If You Have Any Doubts?

That's totally fine.
You can close this page, pretend it never happened, and we'll just go back to being fellow Monday travelers — same bus, same road, just from two quiet worlds.

Thanks for making it this far — that already means more than you think.
The decision is 100% yours. Until then, I have to keep faith on math.

— Supriyo
